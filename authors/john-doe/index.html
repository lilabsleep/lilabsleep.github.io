During the aging process, the brain experiences a significant loss of hypocretin (Hcrt)/orexin neurons. The high excitability of aged hypocretin neurons, characterized by depolarization of the resting potential, along with the adaptive upregulation of prepro-Hcrt mRNA expression, leads to fragmented sleep during aging.
